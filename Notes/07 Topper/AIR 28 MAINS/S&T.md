|**IP Rights**|- - **Introduction**<br>        - Intellectual Property Rights (IPRs) are legal rights that grant creators protection for their inventions, designs, artistic works, and brands.<br><br>- - IP is intangible property that is product of **human intellect,** creativity and innovation.<br>    - India has been ranked 40th out of 53 countries on the Global Intellectual Property Index.<br>    - Global innovation index 2022 - India at 40/132.<br><br>- - **Types of IPR**<br>        - ![S&T](<Z9 Obsidian-files/Media/DOCX/S&T 2.png>)<br>        - Patents -<br>            - Exclusive right, can be granted for product or industrial process having economic significance.<br>            - limited period - e.g. 20 years<br>            - Criteria - novelty, non-obviousness, and utility.<br>                - E.g. Novartis (failed on novelty)<br>            - Granted under Indian patent Act 1970<br>        - Invention<br>            - A new process or product with significant difference from the existing one<br>            - Requirement - Novelty, Non-obviousness, Utility<br>        - Trademark<br>            - Graphical representation to establish distinctively<br>                - E.g.- logo<br>            - Trademark Act, 1999<br>        - Trade Secret<br>            - A device or technique used in manufacturing process having commercial value, competitive advantage.<br>        - Copyrights<br>            - Excusive right for artistic material for producing, publishing etc.<br>            - Copyrights are **automatic upon creation** and do not require registration.<br>        - Geographical Indicators<br><br>- - **Significance of IPR**<br>        - Encourages Innovation and Creativity<br>            - Patent to population ratio- USA- 1:6000, China- 1:25000, India - 1:600000<br>        - Creating a culture of research and development<br>        - Attracting Foreign Direct Investment (FDI): Pharma Companies<br>        - Drives Economic Growth: fostering entrepreneurship, investment, job creation<br>        - Facilitates Technology Transfer and Collaboration<br>            - E.g.
